3 Testing Documents

The google form: In our questionnaire we started of by asking our respondent some questions in an effort to screen our participants, chiefly question 1-4. In our screening we wanted to clarify certain aspects of the respondent mainly in regard to his/her experience browsing the web. The following questions are more important as they give actual feedback as to how the respondent experienced the website. We provide four questions where to respondent chooses one of five numbers expressing their experience. Finally, we gave the respondent the oppertunity to tell us what kind of device they used when browsing the website and what browser, followed by a text-field where the respondent can provide a more detailed feedback if so desired. The two latter questions were not required, as we did not want to force our respondents to provide written feedback.

4 Tester Audience

In in our quest for respondents within our clients desired customer group, we opted for an approach in which we shared the form on various social platforms like Facebook, with people we know ourselves to be qualify as probable users in the future. This approach yielded decent results as we were able to get 22 responders. As far as responses are concerned, the result are as follows:

  1. How old are you?

  2. How confident are you with browsing, shopping, or other online-related tasks?

  3. Which device(s) do you usually use for browsing?

  4. Have you used similar sites before?

  5. Did you find the website easy to navigate?

  6. Was the booking feature intuitive?

  7. Were you able to understand the menu?

  8. How was your overall experience with the site?

5 Testing Platforms

Our website was exposed to several devices and browsers, the fact that our website seemingly worked quite well as can be seen from the results of the questionnaire. We were exposed to devices like iphone, Samsung Galaxy, Huawei p30, Mac, PC, s and ipad's, and browsers such as chrome, safari, edge and explorer. All in all, our site was tested on many of the most popular platforms and fared quite well, hence we can with resonable confidence conclude that our site performs well on the most important platforms.

6 Feedback Summary

Overall our feedback was quite positive, respondents really seemed to enjoy the sleek and mordern design of our website. Users found the flow of the page natural and easy to understand. Some respondents however complained that they needed scroll much more than what they were used to, making navigating the site somewhat cumbersome.

7 Your Response

Considering the mostly positive feedback we were quite satisfied with our work. We did receive some useful insight from the respondents regarding features of which inclusion we did not consider during the design nor construction of the website. The uppermost map is certainly a redundant feature, which in hindsight, should be removed. The reason we opted for its inclusion during the design phase, is tied to its ability to communicate the exceptional location at which the restaurant is located. Secondly, a feature that would automatically redirect the user to his/her e-mail whenever clicking on an e-mail element in the contact section of the page, is probably not a features dearly lacked by many users. The fact that only one respondent mentioned it is probably quite telling. It could, however, be a neat feature, but as of now we have not really considered it's implementation as it may prove technically challenging, it's low importance which we inferred from it not being frequently asked for, and lastly, it may also confuse certain users not expecting to be re-directed, hence finding it an annoyance.

8 Self Reflection

As a whole, the project has given all of us a more wholesome insight into the field of webdevelopment. Working on a larger project aided in developing our familiarity with HTML, CSS and JavaScript. Through searching for solutions and testing code, we felt we gained experience and new skills making us all better web-developers. What tought us the most however was helping eachother and collectivly finding solutions to particularly difficult problems, a good example is the implementation of the map, and features concerning the menu. The biggest takeaway form the project however, was the added experience concerning working in a larger project as a member of a group. We all feel like we gained valuable experience as to how a group should function, and got feel that we were all responsible for the outcome of somthing that affected more just ourselves.
